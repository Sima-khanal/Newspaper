In the bat-and-ball game of cricket, two teams of eleven players compete on a field with a 22-yard (20-meter) pitch in the middle. Each wicket has two bails balanced atop three stumps. In order for the batting team to score one run for each exchange, the striker's objective is to hit the bowled ball and then trade places with the nonstriker. Cricket may be played in a variety of formats, from Twenty20 (often called T20), when teams bat for one innings of 20 overs (each "over" consisting of six fair possibilities for the batting team to score) that take three to four hours, to Test matches that span five days.
